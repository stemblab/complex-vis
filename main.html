Visualization of complex functions

Complex functions can be visualized by mapping their argument and phase to a color model (sometimes called [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domain_coloring, domain coloring]). Here we use the [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HSL_and_HSV, HSLA] color model because it is consistent with HTML canvas. The hue (H) is mapped to the argument (and is fixed). The saturation (S), lightness (L) and opacity (A) are roughly consistent with Velleman's approach [http://www.cs.amherst.edu/~djv/FTAp.pdf, (pdf)], and can be edited.
